My Work

Recent Projects

Phoenix UAV – Design/Build/Fly (DBF) 2025 Competition

The Project

Phoenix is a fixed-wing crop-dusting UAV developed by a multidisciplinary team of Aerospace Engineering students at Cairo University for the UDC DBF 2025 competition.

My Role

As a Mechanical Design & Manufacturing Engineer,

I was responsible for the CAD and structural design of the UAV, focusing on achieving an optimal balance between weight, strength, manufacturability, and aerodynamic requirements. My work included designing the internal fuselage structure, payload release mechanism, wing and tail attachment systems, material selection, and detailed 3D modeling using SolidWorks.

Al'ankaa Rover – Rover Rumble Race (RRR) Competition 2026

The Project

Al'ankaa (العنقاء) is a multi-mission rover built by a 5-person team of first and second-year Aerospace Engineering students for the Rover Rumble Race (RRR) competition, organized by the Space Systems Technology Laboratory (SST Lab). The rover was built around an ESP32 microcontroller and required to autonomously solve a maze, detect color sequences and collect matching objects with a 3-DOF robotic arm, and complete a multi-stage race against strict weight and dimension constraints.

My Role

As System Engineer,

I led the software development, data acquisition, structural design, PCB, manufacturing, and documentation tracks of the project. This included writing the ESP32 firmware (motor control, PID-based maze navigation, color sensing, inverse kinematics for the robotic arm, and Wi-Fi/TCP communication with a LabVIEW ground station), designing and etching the single-layer PCB, and contributing to the rover's mechanical structure and CAD documentation, all while keeping the design within the competition's 900g body / 300g arm weight limits.
